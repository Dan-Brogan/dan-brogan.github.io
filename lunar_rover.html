Lunar Rover Concept Architecture: Robotic Remote-Sensing Scout (R2-S2)

R2-S2: Robotic Remote-Sensing Scout – Lunar Rover Concept

This concept project from USC’s ASTE 527 course proposes R2-S2, a versatile robotic rover designed to assist lunar EVA crews on Artemis missions. It operates in two modes:

  • Teleoperated mode – remotely assesses EVA mission safety and conducts tasks that would otherwise require crewed operations.
  • Autonomous crew-assist mode – supports astronauts during EVAs by livestreaming operations, carrying tools, collecting samples, and performing in-situ sensor tasks.

R2-S2 is designed for equatorial and polar missions, with configurable solar panels, wheel width, and battery insulation. Its primary goal is to reduce mission risk and enhance crew safety and productivity on the lunar surface.
